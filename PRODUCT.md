# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Engineering leaders and founders (VP Eng, CTO, platform/infra leads) at startups and mid-size companies, mostly in Metro Atlanta and the US. They're evaluating whether to hire a senior DevOps / platform consultant to fix cloud cost, reliability, security, or delivery-speed problems they can't staff internally. They arrive skeptical, technical-adjacent, and short on time — they want fast proof of seniority and relevant outcomes.

## Product Purpose
A personal consulting site for Ofir Shtrull, operating under the FTC-Cloud brand. It exists to convert qualified visitors into intro calls. Success = a credible, senior first impression that makes a technical buyer trust Ofir with production infrastructure, and a frictionless path to contact.

## Brand Personality
Senior, precise, battle-tested. Voice is first-person ("I"), plain-spoken, outcome-focused — no agency fluff, no hype adjectives. Three words: **senior, precise, calm**. The interface should feel like it was built by the kind of engineer you'd trust with your platform: deliberate, technically literate, quietly confident.

## Anti-references
- Generic blue SaaS landing pages (gradient hero, identical icon-card grids, "cutting-edge solutions" copy).
- Agency sites that hide the person behind a corporate "we."
- Navy-and-gold fintech-trust cliché.
- Terminal-costume dev-tool pages where mono everywhere is decoration, not meaning.

## Design Principles
- **Practice what you preach.** A DevOps consultant's site should itself be fast, accessible, and well-engineered — the site is a work sample.
- **Show seniority, don't claim it.** Concrete outcomes, real tools, honest numbers over adjectives like "top-notch" or "cutting-edge."
- **Name the human.** Buyers hire a person; lead with Ofir, keep FTC-Cloud as the wordmark.
- **Engineering-credible, not engineering-costume.** Technical voice (mono labels, terminal motif) used as a deliberate, sparing system that means something — never as wallpaper.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG 2.1 AA: body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1, visible focus rings, keyboard-navigable nav and form, full `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives. Dark default with a coherent light theme via the existing toggle; respects system preference.
